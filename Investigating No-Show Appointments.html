Investigating the Factors Affecting Appointment Attendance¶

Introduction¶

The purpose of this document is to analyse the data based on sheduled medical appointments that were either attended or not by the patient. The data was obtained Kaggle and is based on 100k medical appointments in Brazil.

The analysis in this document attempts to answer the question "What factors affect appointment attendance?" based on the data set provided.

The field descriptions of dataset include:

  1. 'PatientId' - Unique identifier for the patient
  2. 'AppointmentID' - Unique identifier to mark appointment made
  3. 'Gender' - Gender of the patient
  4. ‘ScheduledDay’ - The day the patient set up their appointment.
  5. 'AppointmentDay' - The day patient scheduled their appointment for
  6. 'Age' - The age of the patient
  7. ‘Neighborhood’ - The location of the hospital
  8. ‘Scholarship’ - Whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Família
  9. 'Hipertension' - Whether or not the patient is hypertensive
  10. 'Diabetes' - Whether or not the patient is diabetic
  11. 'Alcoholism' - Whether or not the patient suffers from alcoholism
  12. 'Handcap' - Whether or not the patient is handicapped
  13. 'SMS Received' - Whether or not the patient receives an SMS about the appointment
  14. 'No-show' - Whether or not the patient attends the appointment

#defining a function to aid with the calculation of proportions for each grouping 
def proportion(data_frame, ind_column, dep_column, ind):
    """The purpose of this function is to calculate the proportions of unique values of the independent variable/column 
    while filtering based on the dependent variable/column """
    tot1 = data_frame.groupby(ind_column)[dep_column].value_counts()
    tot2 = data_frame.groupby(ind_column)[dep_column].count()
    tot3 = tot1[ind]
    prop3 = tot3/tot2[ind]
    return(prop3)

Conclusion¶

Results¶

The main purpose of this analysis is to answer the question "What factors affect appointment attendance?", 4 variables were investigated;

  1. Gender
  2. Existence of Disease
  3. SMS received
  4. Scholarship

Based on the analysis, it was deduced that the gender of patients had no effect on no show as it followed the general no show trend.

The effect of the disease of the patient on no show appointments varied with the type of diseases the patient had. Patients with Hypertensions and/or Hypertension with Alcoholism had the best attendance of all disease groups/combinations. This suggests ailments are pretty serious and the patients the tend to prioritize treatment of their ailments via appointments. People with alcoholism also followed similar trend to that of the general no-show trend and take less priority of their treatment via appointments. This suggests that alcoholism may not be seen as serious as the patients with it and/or the patients use alternative treatment methods that don't require a visit to the doctor.

The data also, showed an unexpected trend for no show appointments when SMS is received. The appointments where SMS was received had a worse attendance than those who had not receive SMS. The number people that receive SMS are around 30% and may be attributed to them opting to receive an SMS possibly because they maybe forgetful. So going along that line, their forgetfulness may have contributed to missing their appointments

Finally scholarship, people with scholarship show a worse attendance than those who pay for medical services. This may be due to the fact, the people who pay for medical services will likely not want to pay penalties for missing their appointments, hence lower no show appointments when compared with those who are sponsored, who will likely not pay any penalties for missing their appointments.

Limitations¶

The major limitation of the data is that variables provided cannot adequately predict why patients will not show up for appointments and more information is required to make better conclusions. For example, with the disease groups, the purpose of the appointment may inform a better judgement as to why the patients did not attend their appointments as patients may schedule appointments for other purposes outside their ailment(s). Another example is the SMS received, the dataset does provide data that could be further explored to know why turnout was worst despite receiving SMS.
